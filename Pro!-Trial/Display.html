Ferns And Their Relatives

Ferns is the numerous Phylum Ferns and their relatives have true roots, leaves and stems

Characteristics

  • Simple
  • Reproductive structure are the most important characteristics for classifying ferns
  • The Sporangia on the underside are grouped into various shapes and patterns
  • Sporangia can be grouped together into circles, elongated along the veins, or arranged around the margins of the frond. There are numerous patterns and variations

Ferns: Nonflowering vascular plants

  • Spore-bearing Leaves
  • Horizontal underground stems
  • 1-2 Feet tall
  • Tropical Ferns may grow as tall as 60 ft. with fronds 12-14 ft.
  • Some are epiphytes

Parts of a Fern

  • Fronds - Leaves
  • Rhizome - A creeping or underground stem, which produces roots
  • Sori - Groups of spore-bearing sporangia (means a heap)
  • ○ Sporangia (Reproductive structure) are protected by little membranous structure (an indusium)
  • ○ These characteristics help us to sort out which family and genus the ferns belong

Sporophyte:The large diploid phase of the plant cycle. Lives independently from gametophyte

  • Cells on the underside of the fern's leaves undergo meiosis and produce haploid Spore; These spores are then released and disperse.
  • Each spore divides and develop into small multicellular Gametophyte
  • Some of the haploid cells on the underside of the gametophyte develops into eggs,, either into sperm,

Gametophyte:The small haploid generation of the fern life cycle; independently from the sporophyte.

  • Sperm swim to the eggs to fuse to form the diploid embryo that divides and develops into an individual of the sporophyte generation. The sporophyte grew out of, but quickly becomes larger than and nutritionally independent from the gametophyte.

Sporophyte Generations of Ferns

  • A new fern's life cycle typically begins in July with the appearance of sori in the undersurface or along the margins of the frond.
  • 1. Sori may be round, kidney-shaped, oblong, linear, curved, or Star-shaped.
  • 2. Ferns and other vascular plants have a life cycle in which the diploid sporophyte is the dominant stage.

Life Cycle of Ferns

  • Fern Sporophytes develop into spores on the underside of their fronds in structure called Sporangia
  • Sori may be round, kidney-shaped, oblong, linear, curved, or Star-shaped.

Sporophyte Generation

  • Sori appears on the underside of the frond.
  • Sori first open and discharged millions of spores.

Gametophyte Generation

  • Spore develops into a tiny green, heart-shaped structure called a prothallus.
  • Seldom seen, only 1 cell layer in thickness.
  • The underside of the prothallus develops archegonia and antheridia
  • ○ Antheridia near the point (Sperm)
  • ○ Archegonia at the notched end (ova)
  • Sperm are released from the antheridia and swim to the ovum at the bottom of the archegonia.

Sporophyte Stage

  • The zygote matures and sends the first leaf up and the first root down.
  • The First Leaf is often a fan-shaped blad
  • The second leaf is usually a Fiddlehead, a coiled young leaf. Some Fiddlehead are edible and used in salads.
